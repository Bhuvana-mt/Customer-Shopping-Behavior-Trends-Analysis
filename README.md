# 🛍️ Customer Shopping Behavior & Trends Analysis
### End-to-End Data Analytics Project | Python · PostgreSQL · Power BI

---

## 📌 Problem Statement

A leading retail company wants to better understand its customers' shopping behavior in order to improve sales, customer satisfaction, and long-term loyalty. Management observed shifting purchasing patterns across demographics, product categories, and sales channels, and needed data-driven answers to a core business question:

> **"How can the company leverage consumer shopping data to identify trends, improve customer engagement, and optimize marketing and product strategies?"**

---

## 🎯 Project Objective

Analyze a retail consumer behavior dataset of 3,900+ transactions to uncover:
- Which customer segments and demographics drive the most revenue
- How discounts, promo codes, and subscriptions affect purchase behavior
- Which product categories and items perform best by season and location
- What factors influence customer loyalty and repeat purchases
- Actionable recommendations the business can act on immediately

---

## 🔄 Project Workflow

### 1️⃣ Data Cleaning & EDA — Python

- Loaded and inspected the raw dataset (shape, dtypes, value distributions)
- Handled missing values in `Review Rating` (imputed with median)
- Detected and resolved an invalid entry in the `Gender` column
- Validated ranges for `Age`, `Purchase Amount`, and `Review Rating`
- Checked for duplicate `Customer ID` entries
- Performed univariate and bivariate analysis across all key dimensions
- Visualized distributions, category breakdowns, and seasonal patterns

### 2️⃣ Business Analysis — SQL (PostgreSQL)

Wrote 10 structured SQL queries to answer real business questions:

| # | Business Question |
|---|---|
| 1 | Total revenue generated by male vs. female customers |
| 2 | Customers who used a discount but still spent above average |
| 3 | Top 5 products by highest average review rating |
| 4 | Average purchase amount — Standard vs. Express shipping |
| 5 | Do subscribed customers spend more? Revenue and avg spend comparison |
| 6 | Top 5 products with highest discount purchase rate |
| 7 | Customer segmentation — New, Returning, Loyal (CTE + CASE WHEN) |
| 8 | Top 3 most purchased products within each category (Window Function) |
| 9 | Are repeat buyers (5+ purchases) more likely to subscribe? |
| 10 | Revenue contribution by age group |

### 3️⃣ Dashboard — Power BI

Built an interactive multi-page dashboard featuring:
- KPI cards: Total Revenue ($233K), Avg Purchase ($59.76), Avg Rating (3.75/5)
- Revenue breakdown by Category, Season, and US State
- Customer demographics: Age distribution, Gender split
- Subscription vs. non-subscription revenue comparison
- Discount & promo code impact analysis
- Payment method and shipping preference distribution
- Dynamic slicers: Season, Category, Gender, Subscription Status

## 📊 Key Findings

- 💰 **$233,081** total revenue across 3,900 transactions · **$59.76** average purchase value
- 🏷️ Customers who received discounts but still spent above average represent a wasted discount budget — they would have purchased anyway
- 🔁 **27% of customers are subscribers** — but a significant number of repeat buyers (5+ purchases) have not yet converted to a subscription
- 📦 Clothing dominates revenue across all seasons; Outerwear peaks in Winter
- 🧩 Customer segmentation (New / Returning / Loyal) reveals three distinct groups that require different marketing strategies
- 🏆 Top-3-per-category product rankings (using SQL window functions) highlight clear inventory priorities
- 📍 Montana, California, and Idaho are the top 3 revenue-generating states

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

1. **Convert repeat buyers to subscribers** — customers with 5+ purchases who are not yet subscribed are a warm, low-cost conversion opportunity
2. **Redirect discount budget** — move discounts away from high-value customers (who spend above average regardless) toward mid-tier customers where discounts can actually change behavior
3. **Prioritize stock by category winner** — use top-3-per-category findings to guide inventory restocking and bundling decisions
4. **Leverage seasonal demand** — align marketing campaigns and product promotions with category-specific seasonal patterns
5. **Invest in the Loyal segment** — customers with 10+ purchases deserve a formal loyalty tier with exclusive benefits and referral incentives
